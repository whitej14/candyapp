INFORMATION

MORE ON LEAD

When Americans buy candy from the grocery store, most assume the worst they're risking is tooth decay. But as it turns out, a significant number of candies sold in California stores were recently found to contain harmful amounts of lead, which can permanently damage kids' developing brains.

According to California law, businesses are required to warn customers about when the amount of lead in food exceeds ten parts per billion (0.01 ppm). But figuring out which candies are safe and which are not is trickier than you think. Some companies that used to sell tainted candies have cleaned up their products, while others are facing new sources of contamination in certain ingredients.

Test out your own lead candy IQ by sorting our pile of candy products into the pink and green grocery baskets. Each product's lead value is based on publically available information provided by the State of California for 2013 and 2014.
